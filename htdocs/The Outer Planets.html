Jupiter, the fifth closest planet from the sun and the largest planet in the entire solar system was named after the Roman god who was the king of gods. Due to its size it has approximately 80 moons and possibly even more! As a gas planet, it has a hydrogen atmosphere and mantle but metallic, and a molten rock core. And in its atmosphere, it can have storms with wind speeds reaching 900 km/h.
Fun Fact: Jupiter has a large red storm the size of two earths and has been going on for 150 years!
Fun Fact: Jupiter actually has rings made of dust, therefore it’s hard to see!

Uranus, the second farthest planet from the sun, without counting Pluto, and is known as the sideways planet because it tilted by 90°, was named after the Greek god of the sky. It has 27 moons, 13 inner moons, 5 major moons, and 9 irregular moons. Again, it has a mostly hydrogen atmosphere and mantle but metallic, and a molten rock core. And it has 10 relatively dark rings made of water and ice.
Fun Fact: Uranus’s magnetic pole is actually tilted to its equator by 150° instead of 90°!
Fun Fact: Instead of being a gas giant its actually one of two ice giant because its are mostly comprised of ice!
